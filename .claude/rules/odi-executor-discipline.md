# ODI: Executor Discipline

## One executor per job map

Every outcome in a map must describe the experience of the same person. If outcomes require different people, split into separate maps.

## Consistency test

For each outcome ask: "Is the same person experiencing this?" If the answer shifts between roles, split the map. Watch for these conflation patterns:

- **Governance vs. Operations:** "Oversee return filing" and "Register in new states" are different executors
- **Strategy vs. Execution:** "Define compliance risk tolerance" and "Flag invoices for review" are different executors
- **Professional vs. Non-professional:** If some outcomes assume domain expertise and others assume the executor lacks it, you're serving two executors

## Emotional and social jobs are required

Every functional job map must include emotional and social jobs. These often drive solution adoption more than functional outcomes.

**Emotional jobs** — how the executor wants to feel or avoid feeling:
- Format: `[Feel/Avoid feeling] [emotional state] [when/while context]`
- Example: "Avoid feeling anxious that I'm creating hidden liability through invoices I'm not reviewing"

**Social jobs** — how the executor wants to be perceived:
- Format: `[Be seen as / Avoid being seen as] [perception] [by whom]`
- Example: "Avoid being blamed when an audit reveals years of missed accruals"

Emotional/social jobs matter most when the executor is working outside their training, when failure consequences are delayed, when they have accountability without authority, or when leadership visibility is involved.
